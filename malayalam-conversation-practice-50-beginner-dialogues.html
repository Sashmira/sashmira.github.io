Malayalam conversation practice: 50 beginner dialogues

Use these prompts to practise spoken Malayalam with a tutor, family member, AI tutor, voice note or mirror practice.

Practise with prompts

50 beginner dialogue prompts

  1. Greet someone and ask how they are.
  2. Introduce yourself in two sentences.
  3. Ask where your mother is.
  4. Ask if father has eaten.
  5. Tell grandmother you are fine.
  6. Ask for water politely.
  7. Say you want tea.
  8. Say you do not want rice.
  9. Ask where the shop is.
  10. Ask how much something costs.
  11. Tell someone you are going home.
  12. Say you are coming now.
  13. Ask someone to say that again.
  14. Say you do not understand.
  15. Say you know a little Malayalam.
  16. Ask what this is.
  17. Ask who is there.
  18. Ask when they are coming.
  19. Ask why they are late.
  20. Ask how to go there.
  21. Talk about your morning.
  22. Talk about what you ate.
  23. Say what food you like.
  24. Say you are studying Malayalam.
  25. Say you are working today.
  26. Ask if someone is at home.
  27. Invite someone to sit.
  28. Thank someone politely.
  29. Say sorry politely.
  30. Ask for help.
  31. Ask for directions.
  32. Ask for the bill.
  33. Talk to an auto driver.
  34. Ask where the bus stop is.
  35. Say you are feeling happy.
  36. Say you are tired.
  37. Ask if someone is angry.
  38. Give a simple compliment.
  39. Talk about a family visit.
  40. Talk about a Kerala trip.
  41. Ask about school.
  42. Ask about work.
  43. Ask someone to call later.
  44. Leave a simple voice message.
  45. Say good night.
  46. Say see you later.
  47. Ask a tutor to correct you.
  48. Repeat a sentence slowly.
  49. Tell someone your goal.
  50. End the conversation politely.

How to practise

Pick five prompts. Speak each one slowly. Record yourself. Then compare your sentence with a tutor, the AI tutor prompt page, or the spoken Malayalam method.
