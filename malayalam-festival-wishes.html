Onam, Vishu, Birthday & Festival Wishes in Malayalam

By Dr. Reshmi R Nair, PhD · Updated June 2026

Nothing delights a Malayali family like receiving festival wishes in Malayalam — especially from someone still learning. Here are the greetings for every big occasion, in easy Romanised Malayalam, ready for your next call or WhatsApp message.

The magic word: ashamsakal

Ashamsakal means “wishes / greetings” — attach it to almost any occasion and you have a perfect festival greeting. Master this one word and you can celebrate everything.

Onam — Kerala’s biggest festival

Onashamsakal! — Happy Onam!  ·  Ellavarkkum ente hridayam niranja Onashamsakal — Heartfelt Onam wishes to everyone

Onam (August–September) celebrates King Mahabali’s yearly visit — ten days of flower carpets (pookkalam), the grand Onam sadya feast, and family reunions. One warm Onashamsakal in a family WhatsApp group earns instant affection.

Birthday wishes that feel personal

Janmadinashamsakal! — Happy birthday!  ·  Orupaadu orupaadu janmadinashamsakal — Many, many happy returns

For children and close family, Malayalis often add blessings: nanma varatte (may goodness come to you) or eeshwaran anugrahikkatte (may God bless you). Adding one of these after your wish makes it feel truly native.

Vishu in one minute

Vishu (mid-April) opens the Malayali new year with the Vishukkani — the first auspicious sight of the morning: golden konna flowers, rice, fruit and a mirror arranged the night before. Elders gift children Vishu kaineettam (money). Your greeting: Vishu ashamsakal!

Frequently asked questions

How do you wish Happy Onam in Malayalam?

Say "Onashamsakal!" For a fuller blessing: "Ellavarkkum ente hridayam niranja Onashamsakal" — heartfelt Onam wishes to everyone.

How do you say happy birthday in Malayalam?

"Janmadinashamsakal" (or "pirannal ashamsakal") means happy birthday. Add "nanma varatte" — may goodness come to you — to make it warmer.

What does ashamsakal mean?

Ashamsakal means wishes or greetings. Attach it to any occasion — Onashamsakal (Onam), Vishu ashamsakal, Christmas ashamsakal, Puthuvalsara ashamsakal (New Year).

How do you wish Eid in Malayalam?

"Perunnal ashamsakal" is the Malayalam way to wish Eid Mubarak — perunnal is the Malayalam word for the Eid festival.
