Why a linguist beats an app

There is no Duolingo for Malayalam — and even the apps that exist only build vocabulary, never real speech. Here is the difference:

  • You speak the whole lesson. Real-time correction fixes pronunciation before bad habits set.
  • Everything in clear English. The method is English-maximum, so nothing gets lost in translation.
  • Spoken-first, script-later. Start with romanised Malayalam and speak from day one. Add the script when you want it.
  • Patterns, not memorisation. Learn frames like “Enikku ___ venam” (I want ___) and generate dozens of sentences instantly.
  • Built around your goal. Family, travel, work or heritage — your lessons are yours.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to learn the script first?

No. You start speaking immediately in romanised Malayalam. The script comes later, only if you want it.

Can a complete beginner do this?

Yes — most learners start from zero. The pattern-based method is built for adult beginners.

What time zones are available?

Lessons run across time zones to suit learners in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Europe and the Gulf.

How long until I can hold a conversation?

With one or two lessons a week plus light practice, most learners reach simple conversations within a few months. Your pace depends on practice and speaking time.
