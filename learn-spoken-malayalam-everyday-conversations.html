Spoken Malayalam

Learn spoken Malayalam for everyday conversations

The fastest way to start speaking Malayalam is to learn small reusable sentence patterns for real life: family calls, food, travel, shopping, home and daily questions.

Use the spoken method

Start with patterns, not word lists

Words matter, but spoken Malayalam becomes useful when you can place those words inside a sentence. Begin with patterns like I want, where is, I am going, please give, I know, I do not know and say again.

Family

Learn replies for calls, visits, food, health and simple updates.

Travel

Ask where something is, how much it costs and how to go somewhere.

Daily life

Talk about eating, coming, going, liking, needing and asking.

Five starter conversation frames

I want... Use this for water, food, help, tickets, directions and practice.
Where is...? Use this for places, rooms, bus stops, shops and items.
I am going... Use this for home, work, school, temple, shop or Kerala travel.
Please say again. This is one of the most important survival phrases for beginners.
I do not know. A useful honest reply that keeps conversation moving.

Build your everyday set

Use the first 100 Malayalam words with question words, verbs, family words and food words. Then practise with the AI Malayalam tutor prompts or a Malayalam tutor.
