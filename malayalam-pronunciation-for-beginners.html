Pronunciation

Malayalam pronunciation for beginners

Malayalam pronunciation feels difficult because beginners often miss sound length, tongue position and double consonants. A small amount of focused listening can fix a lot.

Open pronunciation guide

Sounds beginners should notice

Long and short vowels

Changing vowel length can change how natural a word sounds. Listen before you repeat.

Retroflex sounds

Some Malayalam sounds use the tongue curled slightly back, unlike most English sounds.

Double consonants

Malayalam often holds consonants longer. Do not rush through them.

Word endings

Many beginner mistakes happen at the end of words, where Malayalam rhythm matters.

A simple practice method

Listen once without speaking. Listen again and clap the rhythm. Then repeat slowly. Finally, use the word inside a short sentence. Pronunciation improves faster when the sound is connected to real speech.
