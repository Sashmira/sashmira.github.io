Everyday spoken Malayalam

Daily-Use Malayalam Sentences

By Reshmi R Nair, Malayalam Linguist · casual, everyday Malayalam — not textbook.

Want the sentences that actually carry a normal day in Malayalam? Here are the casual, spoken lines for greetings, family, food, getting around and feelings - in easy romanised spelling, each linked to audio so you can hear it and say it.

These are the sentences that carry an ordinary day in Malayalam — casual, spoken, and ready to use. Each is in easy romanised spelling. Tap any linked phrase to hear it and see the Malayalam script.

Greetings & small talk

  • Sukhamaano? — How are you? (hear it)
  • Sugham, ninakko? — I'm good, and you? (hear it)
  • Entha vishesham? — What's new?
  • Choru kazhicho? — Have you eaten? (hear it)
  • Pinne kaanaam — See you later (hear it)

At home & with family

  • Njaan veettilaa — I'm at home (hear it)
  • Amma evideyaa? — Where is mom?
  • Vaa, irikku — Come, sit
  • Enikku ninne ishtaanu — I love you (hear it)

Food & tea shop

  • Enikku vishakkunnu — I'm hungry (hear it)
  • Oru chaaya thaa — One tea please (hear it)
  • Erivu kurachu mathi — Just a little spice (hear it)
  • Nalla ruchi! — Delicious! (hear it)
  • Bill tharumo? — Can I have the bill? (hear it)

Out & about

  • ...evideyaa? — Where is...? (hear it)
  • Ethra dooram? — How far is it? (hear it)
  • Ithethrayaa? — How much is this? (hear it)
  • Onnu sahaayikkaamo? — Can you help me? (hear it)

Feelings & learner lines

  • Adipoli! — Awesome! (hear it)
  • Enikku sukhamilla — I'm not feeling well (hear it)
  • Ithiri pathukke parayaamo? — Can you speak slowly? (hear it)
  • Enikku manassilaayilla — I didn't understand
  • Njaan Malayalam padhichondirikkuvaa — I'm still learning Malayalam

Frequently asked questions

What are the most useful everyday Malayalam sentences?

Greetings like Sukhamaano? (how are you?), food lines like oru chaaya thaa (one tea please) and nalla ruchi! (delicious!), and survival lines like ithiri pathukke parayaamo? (can you speak slowly?).

How do I practise Malayalam sentences?

Learn them as whole phrases, then hear them inside real conversations and shadow each line aloud. Ten minutes a day builds fast recall.

Are these formal or casual Malayalam sentences?

All casual, everyday spoken Malayalam - the way people actually talk in Kerala, not formal textbook language.

Do I need the Malayalam script for these?

No - they're written in easy romanised spelling so you can say them right away; the script is optional.
