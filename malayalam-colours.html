Malayalam Colours (Niram)

By Dr. Reshmi R Nair, PhD · Updated June 2026

Putting colour words to work

Colours become practical the moment you combine them with the question ethu niram? (what colour?). Shopping for clothes, describing objects or playing with children all rely on this. You can also stack a colour before a noun — chuvappu pootthu (red flower), neela aakaasham (blue sky) — to build simple, vivid descriptions.

For children, colours are a joyful early win. Point to everyday objects around the house — a manja (yellow) banana, a pacha (green) leaf — and name them in Malayalam. Turn it into an ‘I spy’ game and the words stick without any feeling of study.

Try describing what you’re wearing or what you see on a walk entirely through colour words. This kind of real-world labelling moves the vocabulary from a memorised list into language you can actually reach for in conversation.

Frequently asked questions

How do you say colours in Malayalam?

Common colours include veluppu (white), karuppu (black), chuvappu (red), pacha (green), neela (blue) and manja (yellow). "Niram" means colour.

What does 'niram' mean in Malayalam?

"Niram" means colour. To ask "what colour is it?" you can say "ethu niram?" — handy for shopping and describing things.

Are colour words useful for beginners?

Yes — colours come up in shopping, describing objects and talking with children, so they're a practical, easy early vocabulary set.

How can children learn Malayalam colours?

Through everyday objects, toys and games — pointing to colours around the house and naming them in Malayalam makes them stick quickly.
